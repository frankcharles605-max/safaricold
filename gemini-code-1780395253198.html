FA Cooling Technical Answer Center

1. Why do non-inverter air conditioners consume more KPLC prepaid tokens?

Non-inverter compressors operate on a simple binary on/off profile. Every time the system boots up, it draws a massive inrush electrical surge. This shifts your consumer profile into higher KPLC cost tiers. Smart inverter models from Daikin, LG, and Samsung scale their compressor speeds smoothly, reducing consumption by up to 30-50%.

2. Which system configuration is optimal for managing Lake Victoria's humidity surges?

Midea and Gree tropicalized split units with dedicated dehumidification cycles are ideal. They run low-frequency continuous fan passes to strip moisture out of indoor spaces without over-cooling them.

3. What is the technical advantage of Blue Fin and Gold Fin condenser coatings?

These are epoxy-based anti-corrosive treatments applied directly over the outdoor unit's aluminum heat-exchanger fins. This protective layer prevents high-salinity air along the coast from triggering galvanic rust, extending equipment life significantly.

4. How often should industrial cold room systems undergo full filter and coil clearings?

For high-turnover agricultural and fish processing hubs, air filter maintenance is recommended every 2-4 weeks, alongside quarterly chemical washes for the condenser coil faces. This ensures steady airflow and prevents high head-pressure failures.

5. Can a centralized VRF system integrate different indoor unit types simultaneously?

Yes. A single outdoor inverter multi-split condenser unit can run ceiling cassettes in retail spaces, concealed ducted units in boardrooms, and wall-mounted setups in smaller offices at the same time.
